Lady Augusta Ada Lovelace


Lady Augusta Ada Lovelace 1815-1852

Quick facts:

  • Lord Byron was her father. Although Ada Lovelace was English poet Lord George Gordon Byron’s only legitimate child, he was hardly an exemplary father.

  • Fearing Lovelace would follow in her father’s footsteps, Lady Byron immersed her in mathematics.

  • At the age of 12, Lovelace conceptualized a flying machine.

  • The “father of the computer” was her mentor. At the age of 17, Lovelace met inventor and mathematician Charles Babbage and watched him demonstrate a model portion of his difference engine.

  • She was a visionary who predicted that computers could do more than just crunch numbers.

  • She was a compulsive gambler.

  • Charles Dickens read a passage from one of his novels to Lovelace on her deathbed.

  • Lovelace was buried next to the father she never knew. Although Lovelace didn’t know Lord Byron, she maintained a life-long fascination with him and his works.

  • Her contributions to computing weren’t recognized until a century after her death.
